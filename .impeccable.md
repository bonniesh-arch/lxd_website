## Design Context

### Users
Adult learners in remote contexts using design-thinking activities to spark creativity and generate ideas.

### Brand Personality
Sparking creativity — energetic, experimental, inspiring, and memorable.

### Emotional Goals
- **Bold & memorable**: This should stand out and stick with users
- **Creative & inspiring**: The interface itself should inspire creative thinking

### Aesthetic Direction
Experimental & creative — pushing beyond the safe, minimal defaults while maintaining clarity. The design should feel like a tool for creators, not a corporate dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. **Dramatic scale contrast** — Big moments command attention, small moments recede; no false equivalence
2. **Unexpected color intensity** — Move beyond safe blues; use bold, saturated colors that energize
3. **Asymmetric composition** — Break from centered, balanced layouts; create tension and visual interest
4. **Distinctive typography** — Use fonts and scales that feel intentional and speak to creativity
5. **Progressive disclosure** — Reveal complexity as users engage; start bold with entry points

## Current State Analysis

**Strengths:**
- Quality font stack (DM families) shows intentional design thinking
- Clean, functional layout supports learning flow
- Subtle blue accent keeps things approachable

**Opportunity:**
- Design feels safe and generic—perfect for amplification
- Blue accent is timid (needs saturation, boldness, or complete reimagining)
- Typography scale is subtle—can be more dramatic
- Layouts are balanced and predictable—asymmetry could add energy
- Color palette could be richer and more expressive
